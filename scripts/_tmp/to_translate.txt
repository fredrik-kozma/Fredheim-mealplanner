CATEGORY: Dinner
INGREDIENTS:
  0: 0 1 Onion, yellow, Norwegian, raw
  1: 0 4 Garlic, raw
  2: 0 750 Tofu, soyabønneprodukt
  3: 0 1 Leeks, Norwegian, raw
  4: 0 1 Carrot, Norwegian, raw
  5: 0 2 Nutritional yeast
  6: 0 2 Salt, table salt
  7: 0 4 Soy sauce
  8: 0 1 Olive oil
  9: 0 3 Water, drinking water
  10: 0 2 Cashew, unsalted
  11: 0 1 Turmeric, ground
  12: 0 3 Parsley, Norwegian, raw
STEPS:
  0: Sautée onion, garlic, leek (sliced in rings) and carrots (grated) with 4 tbsp water, soy sauce and oil. Cook until soft.

=== swedish-oven-pancake ===

  7: If it´s not served the same day, simply heat it up before serving.

=== sweet-potato-muffins ===
TITLE: Sweet Potato Muffins
CATEGORY: Dinner
INGREDIENTS:
  0: 450 g Sweet potato, raw
  1: 4.7 dl Oat flour
  2: 1 tsp Cinnamon, ground
  3: 1 tsp Baking powder
  4: 1 tsp Baking soda
  5: 0.75 tsp Sea salt
  6: 1.2 dl Maple syrup
  7: 1.2 dl Coconut oil
  8: 75 ml Non-diary milk
  9: 1 pinch Vanilla powder
  10: 1.2 dl Pecans
STEPS:
  0: Peel th esweet potato and cut in smaller and eaven pieces. Put then in a pot with water and cook until they are soft.
  1: In a large bowl, combine the flour, cinnamon, baking powder, baking soda and salt. Mix the dry ingredients together.
  2: In the vitamix, combine the cooked sweet potato, maple syrup, coconut oil (preferably melted), non-dinary milk and vanilla. Blend on high until totally smooth and creamy.
  3: Pour the pureéd swwet potato mixture into the large bowl with the dry ingredients. Gently stir and fold everything together until JUST combined.
  4: Preheat the oven to 180 degrees celsius.
  5: Put paper baking cups in the metal baking form and fill them with the mixture, approximately half full in each.
  6: Topp them with some chopped pecans as garnish. Press them into the batter so they stick.
  7: Bake the muffins for 20min or until the tops are domed and slightly darkened and a knife inserted into the middle of one comes out clean.
  8: Let the muffins cool down completely before serving.

DESC: Soft, pillowy burger and sandwich buns made with gluten-free flour.
INGREDIENTS:
  0: 500 g Gluten-free bread flour
  1: 300 ml Water
  2: 30 ml Neutral oil
  3: 7 g Salt
  4: 7 g Instant yeast
STEPS:
  0: Make dough following bread recipe with tangzhong method, mixing vigorously for 4-5 minutes until sticky but scoopable.
  1: Divide dough: 110-120g for burger buns or 80-90g for smaller rolls.
  2: Shape with wet hands by gently cupping and rotating dough, smooth top without kneading, place on baking paper.
  3: Cover loosely and rise 30-45 minutes until 50-70% larger.
  4: Optional: brush with oat milk and sprinkle sesame seeds, poppy seeds, or oats.
  5: Bake at 220°C for 8 minutes, then 190°C for 12-18 minutes until golden and hollow-sounding.
  6: Cool at least 30-45 minutes before slicing.

=== fredheim-deluxe-plant-based-cream-cheese ===
TITLE: Fredheim Deluxe Plant-Based Cream Cheese
CATEGORY: Spreads
DESC: Rich and creamy cashew-based cream cheese with fresh herbs.
INGREDIENTS:
  0: 900 g Raw cashews, soaked
  1: 240 g Medjool dates, pitted
  2: 270 ml Fresh lemon juice
  3: 3 tsp Lemon zest
  4: 15 g Salt
  5: 15 g White miso
  6: 60 g Nutritional yeast
  7: 12 g Onion powder
  8: 9 g Garlic powder
  9: 400 ml Cold water
  10: 35 g Fresh chives, chopped
  11: 20 g Fresh dill, chopped
STEPS:
  0: Soak raw cashews in water for 2-4 hours, or pour boiling water and soak 10 minutes, then drain.
  1: Blend in 2-3 batches: combine cashews, dates, lemon juice, lemon zest, salt, miso, yeast, onion powder, garlic powder, and water.
  2: Blend until completely smooth and creamy.
  3: Add water gradually to reach thick but spreadable consistency (typically 300-500ml total).
  4: Transfer to bowl and fold in finely chopped chives and dill.
  5: Refrigerate 2-3 hours before serving; cream cheese will thicken and flavors will develop.

=== creamy-red-lentil-stew ===
TITLE: Creamy Red Lentil Stew
CATEGORY: Dinner
DESC: Warming stew with red lentils, vegetables, and creamy cashew milk.
INGREDIENTS:
  0: 300 g Red lentils, rinsed
  1: 1 pcs Medium onion, finely chopped
  2: 3 pcs Garlic cloves, minced
  3: 1 pcs Large carrot, diced
  4: 2 pcs Small potatoes, diced
  5: 1 pcs Red bell pepper, diced
  6: 1000 ml Vegetable broth
  7: 400 g Crushed tomatoes
  8: 1 tsp Ground cumin
  9: 1.5 tsp Smoked paprika
  10: 0.5 tsp Turmeric
  11: 0.5 tsp Cinnamon
  12: 0.5 tsp Ground coriander
  13: 1.5 tsp Salt
  14: 2 tbsp Date paste
  15: 0.5 pcs Lemon, juice
  16: 120 g Raw cashews for milk
  17: 360 ml Water for milk
  18: 15 g Fresh cilantro, chopped
STEPS:
  0: Saute onions, garlic, carrots, and bell pepper with splash of water over medium heat until soft and fragrant, about 5-7 minutes.
  1: Add cumin, smoked paprika, turmeric, cinnamon, coriander, and salt, stirring 30 seconds until fragrant.
  2: Stir in lentils, potatoes, crushed tomatoes, broth, and date paste. Bring to boil.
  3: Reduce heat to low, cover partially, and simmer 20-25 minutes until lentils and vegetables are soft.
  4: Blend cashews with water until smooth and creamy.
  5: Stir cashew milk and lemon juice into stew, simmer 5 minutes to thicken.
  6: Optional: use immersion blender to blend portion for creaminess while keeping some texture.
  7: Taste and adjust salt, lemon, and spices as needed.
  8: Serve hot garnished with cilantro, alongside bread, rice, or quinoa.

=== ultimate-fluffy-vegan-gluten-free-bread ===
TITLE: Ultimate Fluffy Vegan Gluten-Free Bread
CATEGORY: Bread
DESC: Soft, elastic, moist vegan gluten-free bread that stays fresh for days. Uses psyllium husk for structure and tangzhong method for bakery-level softness.
INGREDIENTS:
  0: 200 g Gluten-free oat flour
  1: 120 g Rice flour
  2: 80 g Tapioca starch
  3: 12 g Psyllium husk powder
  4: 10 g Instant dry yeast
  5: 10 g Salt
  6: 20 g Sugar
  7: 150 ml Water for tangzhong
  8: 40 g Unsweetened applesauce
  9: 15 ml Lemon juice
  10: 315 ml Warm water
STEPS:
  0: Make the tangzhong: Mix 25g oat flour (taken from the 200g) with 150ml water in a small pot. Cook over medium heat, stirring constantly, until thickened, about 2-3 minutes. Cool to room temperature.
  1: Mix all dry ingredients in a large bowl: remaining 175g oat flour, rice flour, tapioca starch, psyllium husk powder, instant yeast, salt, and sugar. Whisk well to combine.
  2: In another bowl, combine the cooled tangzhong, applesauce, lemon juice, and warm water (38-40°C).
  3: Pour wet ingredients into dry ingredients and mix vigorously for 4-5 minutes until sticky but scoopable. The dough should hold together and slowly relax when shaped, not spread flat.
  4: Oil a loaf tin or line with parchment paper. Transfer dough into the tin and smooth the top with a wet hand.
  5: Cover loosely with plastic wrap or damp cloth and let rise at room temperature for 45-60 minutes until dough rises noticeably above the tin.
  6: Preheat oven to 220°C. Bake for 8-10 minutes at high heat, then reduce to 190°C and bake for 40-45 minutes more until light golden brown.
  7: The bread is done when it sounds hollow when tapped on the bottom. Internal temperature should reach 96-98°C.
  8: Remove from tin and cool on a wire rack for at least 1 hour before slicing. The crumb will continue to set as it cools.
  9: Store in an airtight container; bread stays soft and moist for 3-4 days or can be frozen up to 1 month.
